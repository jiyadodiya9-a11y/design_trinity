🔴 About Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in our solar system. It is called the Red Planet because its surface is covered with iron oxide (rust), giving it a distinctive reddish color visible from Earth with the naked eye.

Mars has the tallest volcano in the solar system — Olympus Mons, which stands 21 km high, nearly three times the height of Mount Everest. It also has a massive canyon system called Valles Marineris, which stretches over 4,000 km long and up to 7 km deep.

Mars has a very thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide (95%), which means it cannot support human life without equipment. However, scientists have found evidence of ancient liquid water on Mars, and frozen water ice still exists at its polar caps today. Mars is the most likely candidate for future human colonisation.
