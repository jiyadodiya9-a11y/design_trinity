🪐 About Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere to trap heat, so temperatures swing wildly — scorching hot during the day and freezing cold at night.

Despite being closest to the Sun, Mercury is NOT the hottest planet — Venus is, because it has a thick atmosphere. Mercury's surface is covered with craters, just like our Moon.

Mercury has a huge iron core that takes up 85% of its radius. Surprisingly, scientists have found water ice inside permanently shadowed craters at its poles that never receive sunlight.
